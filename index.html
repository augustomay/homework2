My favourite Brazilian recipe
Let me introduce you to my favourite snack from my country. It uses very simple ingredients and the final result is delicious! I realy hope you will enjoy the results and share with me your experiences or questions!

Ingredients:

  • 500g of sour cassava starch
  • 1 cup of water
  • 1 cup of fresh milk
  • 1 cup of vegetable oil
  • 2 free-range eggs
  • 100 g of grated cheese, traditionally we use Minas cheese, but you can opt for the cheese of your preference, or use no cheese at all
  • A pinch of salt

How to prepare:

  1. In a medium saucepan, boil the water, milk, oil and salt.
  2. Blanch the powder (by pouring hot water) until you manage to knead, that is, to form a dough.
  3. When the dough is cooler, add the cheese and the free-range eggs and mix altogether.
  4. Place the dough in small portions on the oven dish with the aid of a tablespoon, or, if you prefer, soak your hands and shape it carefully into golf balls.
  5. Keep a good space between the portions of dough on the dish, they grow a lot while baking.
  6. To know when to remove them from the oven, observe when they are golden brown on the surface.
  7. Enjoy them immediately, they get too dry after cooling down completely.
This recipe is a very traditional Brazilian mid-afternoon snack, usually eaten with a good cup of coffee. The name of this recipe means "cheese bread", but its main ingredient is actually polvilho azedo, also known in English as sour cassava starch or sour tapioca starch - which is an incorrect name for it. There are many variations for this recipe around the country, but its origins are associated with the State of Minas Gerais, where pão de queijo is prepared in the most traditional way.
